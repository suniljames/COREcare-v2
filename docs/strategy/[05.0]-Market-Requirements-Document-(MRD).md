## 1. Executive Summary

- **The Problem:** US private-duty home care agencies (20,000+ non-Medicare, ~16,200 of them in the 5–200 ADC band `[unverified; derived from narrow SAM]`) are running operations on software architected for desktop-charting workflows and admin-facing buyers — while their highest-volume, highest-turnover user group is the caregiver in the field. The #1 operational pain in the industry is **79% annual caregiver turnover** (narrow analysis §1), and every shred of UX friction in the caregiver mobile experience erodes retention further. Incumbents know this but are structurally stuck: WellSky, AxisCare, AlayaCare, Alora, and CareSmartz360 all shipped mobile apps as a retrofit onto web-first architectures. None of them can cheaply rebuild around mobile-first.
- **The Opportunity:** The private-duty / personal care segment represents a **$3.5–4.5B annual software TAM** (broad analysis §2.1) growing 5–7% annually with only 30–40% digital penetration — the largest underpenetrated segment in home care tech. Narrow SAM = ~16,200 US private-duty agencies in the 5–200 ADC band, with $15K–$25K addressable software spend per agency, representing a ~$240M–$405M direct obtainable software opportunity `[unverified; derived]`. (COREcare's expected mix-weighted ARPA is materially below this addressable-spend ceiling — see [06] for detail.) Three Q1 2026 events sharpen the window: (1) **LivTech's Alora acquisition (April 9, 2026)** opens a 6–12 month displacement window against Alora's 91%-satisfied SMB private-duty base; (2) **AxisCare + Frontier Growth equity** arms the same-tier leader for an offensive push; (3) **Careswitch/Paradigm absorption** creates AI-native-narrative whitespace if Paradigm strips Looper for parts.
- **Target Audience:** US for-profit private-duty / non-medical home care agencies, 5–80 ADC sweet spot (expanding to 200 ADC), owner-operated, predominantly private-pay + LTC insurance + VA payer mix, $15K–$25K annual software budget. Buyer = Agency Owner-Operator or Admin; primary daily users = Caregivers and Office Schedulers; emerging mid-band user = Field Supervisor / Care Manager; customer-experience user = Family Member.
- **Proposed Solution (High-Level Concept):** A caregiver-mobile-first care ops platform — scheduling, visit documentation, and care plan — built on a modern stack (FastAPI + single-Postgres, RLS-enforced multi-tenancy, Clerk RBAC with Family Member as first-class role) that ships with AI documentation assist, audit-first privacy DNA, and transparent published pricing. The mobile caregiver app is the architectural primary surface, not a companion. Back-office is a web admin for owners and schedulers.
- **Key Business Goals:**
  1. Ship v1 to first paid customers by **H2 2026** — scoped tightly to the SMB sweet spot (5–80 ADC).
  2. Reach **30–60 paid agencies / $300K–$540K ARR by end of Year 1** post-launch (Base–Aggressive scenarios per [06] §8.2), with Alora displacement as the primary outbound motion. Mix-weighted ARPA expectation: ~$8K–$10K Y1 expanding to ~$11K–$14K Y3 — see [06] §8 for tier-mix detail.
  3. Prove the caregiver-mobile-first wedge with leading indicators: **caregiver WAU ≥ 70%**, **visit clock-in completion rate ≥ 95%**, **time-to-live ≤ 14 days** `[unverified; aspirational]`.

## 2. The Market Problem & Opportunity

### 2.1. Problem Statement

- **What is the specific, acute pain point?**
  - **Caregiver turnover (79% industry, source §1)** — the operational nightmare every agency owner names first. Every caregiver who leaves costs the agency recruiting dollars, replacement training, client-relationship damage, and lost-shift revenue. Software that adds mobile-side friction is directly complicit.
  - **Documentation burden** — caregivers in the field struggle with non-mobile-native tools to complete visit notes, ADL checklists, and non-clinical observations. The broad analysis quotes field clinicians: *"electronic charting is the scourge of nurses."* On non-mobile-first tools, this translates to after-shift data entry, missed documentation, and billing/compliance risk.
  - **Family communication friction** — #3 pain in the ICP profile (narrow analysis §2.3). Families want visibility into parent/loved-one care. Phone-tag with the scheduler is the current-state norm for many SMB agencies.
  - **Fragmented back-office** — agencies piece together scheduling, credentialing expiration tracking, payroll hours aggregation, and QuickBooks-bound billing workflows across 2–4 tools + spreadsheets. Every tool-boundary crossing is a data-entry-error surface.
- **Who experiences this problem?**
  - **Agency Owner-Operator** (buyer) — bears the P&L consequence of turnover, documentation gaps, and failed state surveys or LTC-insurance audits.
  - **Caregiver** (highest-volume user) — bears the daily cognitive tax of clocking in on tools not built for the field.
  - **Family Member** (customer-experience touchpoint) — churns an agency by complaining, referring poorly, or moving to another provider.
- **Why does this problem exist?**
  - **Architectural path-dependency.** Incumbents built for an era when the agency owner was the primary user and the caregiver was a clock-in peripheral. Retrofitting mobile-first would require multi-year rewrites of workflow engines that currently work (narrow analysis §2.6). They won't do it while legacy revenue holds.
  - **Franchise channel capture.** WellSky's 80% franchise lock (source §5) means their product roadmap serves franchise corporate office IT preferences, not independent agencies — and independents are structurally second-class citizens on WellSky.
  - **SMB tier underinvestment by incumbents.** AlayaCare and WellSky optimize for enterprise ACVs; SMB is a price-pressure segment they service without enthusiasm.
  - **AI commoditization just happened.** Between Nov 2025 and April 2026, every Tier-1 vendor shipped a named AI product (AxisCare Intelligence, AlayaFlow + Layla, Alora's AI positioning, WellSky's roadmap). The competitive floor moved. "Caregiver-mobile-first + audit-first + transparent pricing + AI documentation" is the new minimum credible v1 — not "caregiver-mobile-first alone."

**Market Trends (why now):**

- **PE roll-up compressing the SMB tier.** LivTech/Alora is the opening move, not the closing one. The broad analysis tracks ~$2–3B in PE/strategic capital targeting home care tech in 18 months. Expect 1–2 more SMB consolidations in the next 12–18 months — each closes a customer pool but also creates a displacement window.
- **AI has crossed from differentiator to baseline.** Compassus deployed ambient AI to 8,000+ clinicians (Q1'26 addendum). Every Tier-1 vendor has shipped a named AI product. Customer expectation has shifted from "cool if you have it" to "why don't you have it."
- **Caregiver turnover remains structurally unsolved at 79%.** No existing tool has materially moved this number. The implication: a product that genuinely reduces UX friction for the caregiver has a real operational ROI story, not just a marketing one.
- **Private-pay LTC insurance growth.** Aging demographics + Medicare Advantage benefit expansion = private-pay and hybrid-pay agencies growing faster than Medicare-certified HH (5–7% vs 2–3% CAGR, broad §2.1).
- **CMS HaH waiver extended to 2030** (Q1'26 addendum) — removes a major overhang on the hospital-at-home adjacency, which is v2's longer-horizon expansion lane.
- **Vertical SaaS investor appetite strong for SMB mobile-first plays.** ServiceTitan, Toast, Jobber, Gusto playbook is well-understood; COREcare maps cleanly.

## 3. Target Market & Personas

### 3.1. Primary Target Market

**US for-profit private-duty / non-medical home care agencies, 5–80 ADC (sweet spot), owner-operated or small group, predominantly private-pay / LTC-insurance / VA payer mix, $15K–$25K annual software budget (1–3% of revenue), concentrated in TX / CA / FL / PA / OH (highest HHA density per broad §3).**

Trigger events that put an ICP agency into a buying cycle:
1. Outgrew spreadsheets / CareScheduler-tier tools (most common entry point ~15–25 ADC)
2. Failed-to-fill caregiver-shortage crisis forcing real-time schedule visibility
3. Family complaint cycle forcing a credible portal
4. State survey or LTC-insurance audit forcing an audit trail
5. **NEW in 2026:** Incumbent acquisition (Alora → LivTech; possibly others) forcing a roadmap-risk evaluation

### 3.2. Key User Personas

Five personas for v1. Two primary buyers (Owner, Admin), two primary users (Caregiver, Field Supervisor), one customer-experience user (Family Member). The buyer-vs.-user tension is the central design constraint: owners pay, caregivers adopt. If caregivers don't adopt, the owner churns.

---

#### **Persona 1: "Diana, the Agency Owner-Operator"**

- **Role & Demographics:** 45-year-old woman, former RN or healthcare admin, runs a 35-ADC private-duty agency in suburban Texas she founded 4 years ago. Tech-comfortable but not tech-fluent. Views software as a means to an end, deeply skeptical of vendor promises (has been burned by ClearCare-era migrations before).
- **Core Problem(s):**
  - "I've replaced 28 of my 42 caregivers in the last 12 months. I don't know who's going to show up Monday."
  - "When I lose a shift, it costs me real money — fill-rate is my P&L."
  - "My scheduler spends 15 hours a week on a spreadsheet no one else understands."
  - "I'm three months away from an LTC-insurance audit and I can't prove my care plans were followed."
- **Goals & Motivations:** Operational visibility that actually moves the turnover number. A scheduler tool her admin doesn't fight. An audit trail that doesn't require a fire drill to produce. Transparent software pricing so she can plan her tech budget annually without getting a surprise invoice.
- **"As a… I need to… so that…"** *As an agency owner-operator, I need to see fill-rate, overtime exposure, and caregiver-engagement trends at a glance so that I can act on operational problems before they show up in my P&L.*

---

#### **Persona 2: "Marcus, the Office Admin / Scheduler"**

- **Role & Demographics:** 34-year-old scheduler at a 60-ADC multi-county agency. Works in the back office, lives in the scheduling tool 30+ hours a week, coordinates shift-fills by text, email, and phone. Spreadsheet-expert. Has opinions.
- **Core Problem(s):**
  - "When a caregiver calls out at 6 AM, I have 15 phone calls to make before the client's shift starts. I need to know who's available and credentialed in 30 seconds."
  - "Every new caregiver I onboard has expired credentials or TB tests or CPR cards I find out about 3 weeks in."
  - "I'm the one who hears the family complaints. Nobody's looking at what the caregiver wrote in their visit notes until something goes wrong."
- **Goals & Motivations:** Fill-rate speed. Credential expiration that alerts her *before* the caregiver becomes unschedulable. A scheduling UX that doesn't make her re-type information three times.
- **"As a… I need to… so that…"** *As an office scheduler, I need a caregiver availability + credential status view that updates in real time so that I can fill a call-out in under 15 minutes without calling 10 people.*

---

#### **Persona 3: "Teresa, the Caregiver"**

- **Role & Demographics:** 52-year-old home health aide, 4 years at current agency (above-median tenure). Serves 3 clients on a rotating schedule. Owns an older Android phone. Limited data plan. Often in low-connectivity environments (rural client, apartment basements). Comfortable with familiar apps, impatient with anything that makes her fumble in front of a client.
- **Core Problem(s):**
  - "The clock-in app takes 90 seconds to load and crashes twice a week. My client watches me struggle with it."
  - "I'm writing visit notes from memory at 9 PM on my couch because I couldn't type them during the shift."
  - "I've never met my scheduler in person. I don't know what my pay looks like until it hits my account."
  - "When my license renews, nobody tells me until the scheduler calls to say I can't work tomorrow."
- **Goals & Motivations:** Fast, reliable clock-in that respects her time in front of the client. The ability to document what she did without re-typing everything later. Visibility into her own upcoming shifts, hours, and pay. Respect — tools that don't treat her like a suspect (GPS surveillance is demeaning).
- **"As a… I need to… so that…"** *As a caregiver, I need to clock in, clock out, and complete my visit documentation from my phone in under 60 seconds per task so that I can do my actual job instead of fighting my phone in front of a client.*

---

#### **Persona 4: "Alicia, the Field Supervisor / Care Manager"** *(emerges at ~25+ ADC)*

- **Role & Demographics:** 41-year-old RN or experienced caregiver promoted into a supervisory role. Splits her week between 10–20 client home visits (supervisory / assessment) and office care-plan review. The operational bridge between the scheduler and the caregiver. Owns responsibility for care-plan adherence, incident review, and caregiver coaching.
- **Core Problem(s):**
  - "I do supervisory home visits but the software doesn't know who I am — I show up as a caregiver or an admin, and the permissions are wrong in both directions."
  - "I need to see every visit note for my assigned clients but the tool shows me everything or nothing."
  - "When there's a change in a client's care plan, I don't know if the caregivers in the rotation have actually seen it."
  - "I can't coach a caregiver effectively if I can't see trends in their visits over 60 days."
- **Goals & Motivations:** A role-and-permission model that matches her actual job. Visibility into her caseload without drinking from the firehose. Coaching workflows — not just "did the task get done" but "is this caregiver improving."
- **"As a… I need to… so that…"** *As a field supervisor, I need scoped visibility into the visits, care plans, and caregivers assigned to my caseload so that I can supervise effectively without either losing signal in the noise or being locked out of the data I need.*

---

#### **Persona 5: "Patricia, the Family Member"**

- **Role & Demographics:** 58-year-old daughter of the 82-year-old client. Lives in a different state. Pays the private-pay invoice. Works full-time. Wants visibility into what's happening with her mother without adding a 15-minute phone call to her day.
- **Core Problem(s):**
  - "I don't know if the caregiver actually showed up today until I call my mom."
  - "When Mom's care plan changes — a new med, a new fall precaution — I find out a week later."
  - "I trust the agency, but I want proof — not a phone tree."
- **Goals & Motivations:** Passive, read-only confidence. Not a clinical portal. A sense that the agency is competent and that her mother is being seen by real, qualified people who showed up on time.
- **"As a… I need to… so that…"** *As a family member, I need a simple view of visit completion, care plan updates, and caregiver identity so that I can be confident my parent is being cared for without micromanaging the agency.*

## 5. Market Requirements

### 5.1. Prioritization Key

- **P1 (Must-Have):** Critical to solving the core problem. Non-negotiable for v1 paid launch. Missing any P1 is a deal-breaker in ICP evaluation.
- **P2 (Should-Have):** High-impact requirement that addresses a key user need. Ships within 90 days of v1 GA, or as part of v1 if timeline allows.
- **P3 (Nice-to-Have):** Provides value but is not essential for the core solution. Fast-follow or v1.x.

### 5.2. Requirements by Theme

---

#### **Theme 1: Trust, Onboarding & Speed-to-Live**

*ICP agencies have been burned by slow, painful migrations (ClearCare, Alora-era onboardings). Trust is built in the first 14 days; lost in the first 14 hours.*

- **[P1] Problem:** ICP buyers are skeptical of new vendors and fear a multi-month migration that disrupts live caregiver scheduling.
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an agency owner-operator, I need to see transparent published pricing, a clear list of what's included, and a time-to-live commitment before I schedule a demo so that I can evaluate COREcare without a sales-gated gauntlet.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an agency owner-operator, I need a guided onboarding that gets my caregivers clocking real visits within 14 days of contract signature so that I don't lose operational continuity or burn my scheduler's time on a migration that never ends.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an agency owner-operator evaluating a switch from Alora or WellSky, I need a credible data-migration path for clients, caregivers, schedules, and care plans so that I'm not manually re-entering 18 months of operational history.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As an agency owner, I need to see social proof — customer testimonials, SOC 2 report availability, BAA readiness — during the evaluation so that I can justify the switch to my business partner / spouse / board.*
- **Market Requirement:** v1 must establish buyer trust through transparent pricing, auditable credibility artifacts (SOC 2, HIPAA posture, BAA), and an onboarding commitment that is materially faster than every major incumbent.

---

#### **Theme 2: Caregiver Mobile Experience (the moat)**

*This is the lane's architectural differentiator. Retrofit-mobile competitors cannot match v1 here without multi-year rewrites. Every v1 design decision should prioritize mobile UX over admin UX when they conflict.*

- **[P1] Problem:** Caregivers experience daily mobile-side friction — slow app load, unreliable clock-in, post-shift data entry from memory — that contributes to the 79% industry turnover and corrupts the visit record itself.
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need to clock in to a shift from my phone in under 10 seconds, even on a weak connection, so that I start my visit without fumbling in front of the client.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need a non-GPS visit verification mechanism (signature, photo, NFC tag, or Wi-Fi fingerprint) that respects my privacy while still proving I was at the visit so that I'm not surveilled as a condition of employment.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver working in a basement apartment or rural home, I need the mobile app to work offline and sync when I regain connectivity so that lost signal doesn't mean lost documentation.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need to see my upcoming shifts, hours-to-date, and assigned clients on my phone's home surface so that I don't need to call the scheduler for information I should have.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need to complete ADL checklists, non-clinical vitals, and structured notes from the visit itself (not from memory at 9 PM) so that documentation matches reality.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need to message my scheduler or supervisor from within the app with PHI-safe boundaries so that I don't have to use my personal text message for work coordination.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need shift-swap request and confirmation workflows in-app so that when life happens I can propose a swap without a phone tree.*
- **[P3] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need to see a pay-stub-to-date and earned-wage view in the app so that I know what I've earned without waiting for the next cycle.*
- **Market Requirement:** The mobile caregiver experience must be the platform's architectural primary surface — native-feel, offline-capable, non-GPS-based, and measurably faster for core tasks (clock-in, documentation) than the nearest Tier-1 competitor's retrofit app. Publishing task-level latency benchmarks is a reasonable v1 marketing asset.

#### **Theme 3: Shift Management & Care Operations**

*The scheduler (Persona 2, Marcus) lives here. v1 must beat spreadsheets on speed and match AxisCare on capability.*

- **[P1] Problem:** Schedulers lose 10–20 hours/week to manual availability-matching, credential verification, and call-out fill workflows.
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an office scheduler, I need a single-screen shift-planning view showing caregiver availability, credentials, client match, and distance so that I can assign shifts without tab-switching across three tools.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an office scheduler, I need to see caregiver credential status (license, CPR, TB test, background-check expiration) against every potential assignment so that I don't schedule an unschedulable caregiver.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an office scheduler, I need recurring-shift patterns (weekly, bi-weekly, every-other-weekend) with exception handling so that I'm not rebuilding the same schedule every week.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an office scheduler handling a 6 AM call-out, I need to broadcast the open shift to eligible, credentialed, geographically-matched caregivers and capture first-accept in-app so that I'm not making 15 phone calls before 7 AM.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an office scheduler, I need automatic conflict detection (double-booking, overtime exposure, credential-expired, client-caregiver-mismatch) so that I don't publish a schedule that breaks at runtime.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As an office scheduler, I need caregiver shift-preference and availability self-service (caregivers update their own availability in-app) so that I'm not transcribing availability from text messages into a spreadsheet.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As an agency owner, I need a fill-rate dashboard (target vs. actual, with trend) so that I can see operational health without asking for a report.*
- **[P3] User Story:** *As an office scheduler, I need SMS notifications for critical shift events (caregiver acceptance, client cancellation, credential expiring in 7 days) so that I'm not dependent on email or in-app alone.*
- **Market Requirement:** v1 must deliver scheduler productivity that is 3–5x spreadsheet baseline `[unverified; aspirational]` and at parity with AxisCare on shift-planning capability, without inheriting AxisCare's web-first UX debt.

#### **Theme 4: Visit Documentation & AI Assist**

*The competitive floor on AI moved in Nov 2025–April 2026. v1 ships with AI — not a promise — but leads with audit-and-privacy discipline, not model-size marketing.*

- **[P1] Problem:** Caregivers struggle to complete structured documentation during the visit, leading to after-hours re-creation, gaps, and compliance risk. Simultaneously, every Tier-1 incumbent now markets AI documentation.
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need to dictate or type a short unstructured note that gets structured by AI (into ADL checklist entries, non-clinical vitals, observation categories) so that I'm capturing the truth of the visit, not writing a report.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need AI-generated documentation to be reviewable and editable before submission so that I own what's in my own record.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an agency owner, I need all AI-assisted documentation to be routed through PHI redaction, logged in an auditable structure, and tagged with model + prompt version so that I can defend every AI-generated note in an audit.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As an agency owner, I need an explicit audit-log view of what AI touched what data and when so that state survey and LTC-insurance auditors can verify the integrity of the record.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need a structured visit-documentation template per care plan that enforces task completion without blocking unstructured observation so that the documentation matches both billing requirements and reality.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need AI-surfaced anomaly flags (sudden ADL decline, fall event mentioned in note, caregiver-reported client distress) so that clinical escalations don't get buried in 200 lines of daily notes.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As an agency owner, I need agentic suggestions (caregiver X is disengaging — here are three interventions) that respect my ability to accept, modify, or reject so that AI augments my operational judgment without replacing it.*
- **[P3] User Story:** *As a caregiver, I need voice-to-text documentation that works hands-free during task execution so that I can document while I work.*
- **Market Requirement:** v1 ships with structured visit documentation + AI-assisted note-to-structure transformation + agentic retention/operational suggestions on day one. PHI redaction, audit logging, and per-task model selection are non-negotiable quality gates — not differentiators, but credibility-preservers.

#### **Theme 5: Care Plan, Supervision & Field Oversight**

*Persona 4 (Alicia, Field Supervisor) lives here. This theme is the 25+ ADC credibility gate.*

- **[P1] Problem:** At 25+ ADC, the owner stops scheduling directly and a supervisor emerges, but existing tools have no role-model fit for this person — they are miscast as either an admin with too much access or a caregiver with too little.
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need a distinct role in the platform with scoped permissions (my assigned caseload + my supervisees, not the entire agency) so that I see my accountability surface without being a super-admin.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need structured care plans (goals, interventions, tasks, frequencies) that caregivers see in the context of each shift so that care plan changes don't get lost in email.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need visibility into whether caregivers on my caseload have acknowledged care plan changes so that compliance is traceable.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need my own supervisory-visit documentation workflow (distinct from a caregiver visit) so that oversight is captured and not confused with routine visits.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need caregiver-level trend views (documentation completeness, visit punctuality, client-reported satisfaction) over 30/60/90 day windows so that I can coach proactively.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As a field supervisor, I need incident-capture workflows (fall, ED visit, family complaint) with escalation paths that route to the owner so that critical events don't live in someone's inbox.*
- **Market Requirement:** v1 must support a Field Supervisor role with scoped, caseload-aware RBAC; structured care plans with caregiver-acknowledgment tracking; and a supervisory-visit documentation surface distinct from routine caregiver visits.

#### **Theme 6: Family Communication & Transparency**

*Table stakes, not headline. v1 must match competitor depth but not over-invest here.*

- **[P1] Problem:** Families want visibility and confidence but without phone-tag. Competitor portals range from absent (ShiftCare) to light (WellSky) to decent but retrofit (Alora, AlayaCare).
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a family member, I need to see when the caregiver arrived, when they left, and a high-level summary of the visit so that I have passive confidence without needing to call my parent.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a family member, I need to know who my parent's caregivers are (name, photo, credentials-verified indicator) so that I know real, qualified humans are in my parent's home.*
- **[P1] User Story:** *As a family member, I need notifications when my parent's care plan changes (new medication, fall precaution, supervisor note) so that I'm informed without being surprised.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As a family member, I need to securely message the agency scheduler or supervisor from within the portal so that my communication has an auditable record.*
- **[P2] User Story:** *As an agency owner, I need to control what family members can see per client (some families get more, some less, some none) so that portal visibility respects client-level consent.*
- **[P3] User Story:** *As a family member, I need a read-only view of visit documentation (with appropriate redaction) so that I can verify care delivery for my own peace of mind.*
- **Market Requirement:** v1 must ship a credible, mobile-responsive family portal with visit-arrival visibility, caregiver identity + credentials, and care-plan change notifications — matching competitor depth while avoiding over-investment that would slow the caregiver-mobile moat.

*The detailed pricing model — tier prices, overage structure, complaint-corpus mapping, defensibility analysis, ARPA modeling, and 3-year financial projections — is governed by **[06] Pricing Strategy.md**. **Architectural commitments (binding on v1 product and GTM):**

- **Published, transparent, tiered subscription** on a single page. No "contact sales" gauntlet for any standard tier. Direct attack on the custom-quote opacity that defines AxisCare, AlayaCare, Aaniie, CareSmartz360, and Alora.
- **Per-active-client billing unit, not per-caregiver-seat.** Eliminates the per-seat pattern that quietly punishes customers for the 79% caregiver turnover documented in §2.1. Enforces the lane thesis: the workforce is high-churn; the customer should not pay for that churn.
- **Unlimited staff, scheduler, supervisor, and family-member seats at every tier.** Direct attack on ShiftCare's $9–$25/staff floor and on the family-portal-as-add-on pattern. Has product implications: every seat-class must work at scale without billing pressure constraining usage.
- **Every AI feature included at every tier.** Refuses the AI-as-paid-add-on convergence pattern visible across Toast, Jobber, Gusto, and (likely) the next round of incumbent updates. AI is product DNA, not a SKU. Has product implications for cost engineering — see [06] §5 cost model.
- **No implementation fee; self-serve onboarding default.** Direct attack on ServiceTitan-class $5K–$50K implementation fees and on incumbent 30–90 day go-live timelines. Has product implications for onboarding UX — see Theme 1 of §5.2.
- **Month-to-month default; annual discount opt-in, never required.** Direct attack on multi-year lock-in. Has billing-system implications: subscription must support both cadences with clean proration.
- **Never-raise pricing on existing customers** — published commitment. The single most contrarian element of the pricing architecture and a structural trust signal competitors cannot match without rebuilding their business models. Has product implications: customer record must carry `pricing_version_id` from launch (see [06] §7.6).
- **Annual cost-savings disclosure** — published every January documenting where AI/infra cost reductions went (features, price drops, or margin). Has product implications: cost-attribution telemetry must be in place from launch.

**Strategic posture (one line):** Aggressive-credible vertical-SaaS oxygen-squeeze — capture more agencies at lower ARPA than incumbents, and use trust-architecture commitments to convert price-sensitive evaluators that competitors cannot retain.

## 7. Assumptions, Constraints, & Dependencies

### Key Assumptions

- **AI documentation ships with v1.** The P1 AI requirements assume Anthropic SDK integration, PHI redaction middleware, and audit-logged model-call infrastructure are engineered in time for v1 GA (H2 2026). If AI ships late, the v1 narrative degrades from "AI-equipped at launch" to "AI-promising" — measurably worse competitive positioning.
- **Alora displacement window holds for 6–12 months.** Assumes LivTech's post-acquisition roadmap communication remains vague or customer-dissatisfying through at least Q3 2026. If LivTech ships a clear "nothing changes for Alora customers" communication quickly, the displacement window compresses.
- **No aggressive new AI-native SMB entrant in 2026.** Assumes Paradigm strips Careswitch/Looper or keeps it on a slow trajectory. A Paradigm + Looper-as-platform strategy could close the AI-native narrative whitespace inside 12 months.
- **AxisCare doesn't rebuild mobile-first.** Assumes AxisCare's Frontier Growth-backed offensive spend goes to AI, sales scale, and feature breadth — not an architectural mobile rebuild (which would be multi-year anyway).
- **SMB agencies are willing to trust AI for documentation.** Broad adoption of AI documentation assist assumes owner-operator comfort. Pilot data from incumbents (Compassus 8K+ clinicians) suggests this is reasonable but not certain at the SMB tier.
- **Caregiver turnover continues at 79% industry-wide.** The ROI narrative depends on turnover being a live crisis. A market-wide turnover improvement (regulatory / wage / macro) would weaken the positioning but not eliminate it.

### Constraints

- **HIPAA + state HCBS compliance** — non-negotiable; v1 must ship HIPAA-compliant from day one with BAAs for every subprocessor.
- **US-only geography in v1.** International expansion deferred to v2+ (adjacency).
- **Non-Medicare-certified only.** OASIS-E1 / PDGM / HHVBP out of scope; buyer disqualification is strict.
- **No real-time caregiver GPS** (product anti-pattern). Drives non-GPS visit verification requirement.
- **Team size and velocity.** v1 scope assumes the current team composition and modern-stack velocity; enterprise-tier architectural complexity is explicitly disqualified.
- **H2 2026 launch target.** Missing this compresses the Alora displacement window and risks AxisCare closing the AI-parity narrative gap entirely.

### Dependencies

- **Anthropic SDK (or equivalent LLM provider) reliability and pricing.** AI documentation is P1; provider availability is a single point of failure. Recommend provider-neutral abstraction layer.
- **Data migration tooling.** Alora displacement motion depends on a credible client + caregiver + schedule + care plan migration path. Dedicated engineering effort required.
- **SOC 2 Type II audit timing.** v2 Pro-tier + Mid-Market Pilot buyers will demand SOC 2 within 6–12 months of v1 launch; audit preparation should begin pre-v1-GA.
- **Customer-advisory board.** Pre-launch recruitment of 5–10 design partners from ICP (ideally 2–3 Alora customers openly unhappy with the LivTech deal, 2–3 WellSky independents) is a prerequisite for v1 product-market fit validation.
- **Sales + marketing hiring.** Outbound Alora displacement motion requires at minimum 1 SDR + 1 AE + marketing content ownership by v1 GA.

## 8. Out of Scope (What We Are Not Building)

*Clear boundaries. None of the below are "future v1" — they are structural disqualifications or deferred adjacencies.*

- **Medicare-certified skilled home health workflows** (OASIS-E1, PDGM 30-day episode billing, HHVBP, KLAS-tier certification). HCHB / WellSky enterprise lane. Out of v1, likely out of v2 entirely.
- **Pure RCM / billing-as-a-service.** WellSky RCM, Waystar, ABILITY/Inovalon entrenched at unattainable scale. % of collections revenue model clashes with v1's product-led SaaS posture.
- **EVV aggregator / state Medicaid gateway.** HHAeXchange owns this moat. v1 anti-pattern bars real-time GPS. v1 provides an API handoff to EVV aggregators, but is not itself an EVV system.
- **Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) device platform.** Different category (FDA-cleared hardware, device-centric). No device strategy in v1.
- **Franchise corporate-office offering.** WellSky's 80% franchise channel lock is not a winnable battlefield for v1. Build independent-agency motion.
- **Ultra-small agency sub-$100/month tier.** ShiftCare / Rosemark Starter own this. Unit economics don't support a $20K ARR plan at this band.
- **Enterprise multi-state (200+ ADC) custom workflow engines.** AlayaCare / AxisCare / WellSky territory. Architectural decisions serving this segment would dilute the SMB wedge.
- **Pediatric home care ISP workflows, hospice POC workflows, behavioral health treatment plans.** Care plan extensibility preserved architecturally (per narrow §3), but no v1 workflows built out. Adjacencies for v2+.
- **Senior living / continuing care community management.** Tenant abstraction preserved (per narrow §3), no v1 feature development.
- **International expansion.** US-only in v1. Jurisdictional compliance parameterization preserved architecturally.
- **Hospital-at-home acute-care platform.** Home-side coordination is a v2+ adjacency; acute clinical workflows are out of scope entirely.
- **Value-based contracting (VBC) for HCBS.** Visit-data schema preserves this as a future adjacency; no v1 build.
- **Standalone caregiver training LMS.** CareAcademy-adjacent; integration-yes, build-no.
- **Clinical escalation / telemedicine integration.** HaH adjacency; not in v1.
- **Human-in-the-loop services / white-glove consulting.** Product-led SaaS posture; services revenue not pursued in v1.
