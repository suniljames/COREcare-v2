## Q1. Why now?

Three Q1 2026 events compressed a 24-month strategic window into a 12-month one — and all three point the same direction.

1. **LivTech acquired Alora on April 9, 2026.** Alora has ~1,500 SMB private-duty agencies, 91% CSAT, and a customer base that just got told their roadmap belongs to a PSG-backed roll-up. Two prior PE-acquired-SMB-SaaS playbooks (ClearCare→WellSky, Sandata→HHAeXchange) ran the same script: roadmap stalls 6–18 months, pricing pressure builds, integrations break. The 6–12 month displacement window opens **now** and closes when LivTech ships a credible "nothing changes" communication. (See [02] §1, [04] wave 4.)
2. **AxisCare took Frontier Growth equity on January 29, 2026, on the heels of shipping AxisCare Intelligence in November 2025.** The most threatening direct competitor in our exact ICP band is now both AI-armed and capitalized for an offensive push. The window to plant the flag in our lane before AxisCare's offensive spend hits saturation is the same 12 months.
3. **AI moved from differentiator to baseline between Nov 2025 and April 2026.** Every Tier-1 vendor now markets a named AI product. Compassus deployed ambient AI to 8,000+ clinicians. The competitive floor moved. "Caregiver-mobile-first + AI documentation + audit-first + transparent pricing" is the new minimum credible v1 — "caregiver-mobile-first alone" is no longer enough.

If we ship v1 in H2 2026, all three tailwinds compound. If we slip into 2027, LivTech has stabilized, AxisCare has executed half of its offensive plan, and AI ceases to be a wedge.

## Q3. Why us — what's our durable moat?

Honest split between *moat*, *credibility advantage*, and *parity* (per [01] §2.6):

- **Real moat (18–36 months):** caregiver-mobile-first UX as the primary architectural surface. WellSky, AxisCare, AlayaCare, Alora, Aaniie all have mobile *retrofitted* onto web-first workflow engines. Rebuilding that for any of them is a multi-year project nobody will fund while legacy revenue holds.
- **Real moat (structural, asymmetric):** the trust architecture — never-raise pricing on existing customers + annual cost-savings disclosure. PE-owned incumbents structurally cannot match this without violating their LPs' margin expectations. AxisCare/Frontier, WellSky/TPG, AlayaCare/CIBC are all locked out. (See [06] §1.5, §9.1.)
- **Credibility advantage (12–18 months until matchable):** RLS-enforced multi-tenancy + Clerk RBAC with Family Member as a first-class role + audit-first DNA. Sells in HIPAA conversations; matchable by any motivated competitor.
- **Real moat vs. enterprise / parity vs. SMB tier:** modern stack run-cost. Fully-loaded OpEx is **15–30% of incumbent OpEx per customer** `[EST]` (see [06] §5). Translates to a sustainable price floor incumbents can't match without burning their own ARR.
- **Parity (currently behind):** AI documentation. Resolved in Q5.

The headline pitch leads with caregiver-mobile-first; the trust architecture is the moat that compounds over 36 months and that incumbents cannot touch.

## Q4. AxisCare just got armed. How big a threat? How do we win?

**Recommended position: AxisCare is the most threatening direct competitor in our exact ICP band, and that threat has accelerated. We do not try to out-feature them. We win on three structural advantages they cannot replicate without rebuilding their business model.**

The AxisCare picture in Q2 2026: ~$23.6M revenue, 100+ employees, Frontier Growth equity (Jan 29, 2026), AxisCare Intelligence shipped Nov 2025, strongest API in segment per user reviews, G2 Leader. Their offensive playbook is well-funded and the mid-market 50–150 ADC band is squarely their target. (See [02] AxisCare profile.)

The earlier framing — "AxisCare is a same-tier generalist competitor we can outflank on caregiver mobile" — undersells the threat. With Frontier Growth dollars and AI shipped, they will spend aggressively on outbound, sales scale, and feature breadth through 2026–27. They will publish pricing within 12–18 months. The mid-market band will tighten.

**Three structural counters they cannot copy:**

1. **The never-raise pledge.** AxisCare cannot match this without exposing their existing-customer pricing history (which a PE-backed company structurally cannot do — it would be a tell to the next sponsor about achievable price increases). PE ownership is incompatible with no-price-increase commitments. (See [06] §9.1.)
2. **The annual cost-savings disclosure.** Same conflict. Publishing per-customer infrastructure cost data exposes margin to the same investor class evaluating their next round.
3. **The per-active-client unit on a clean architecture.** AxisCare's entire customer base is on per-deal-quoted contracts. Migrating to a published per-active-client model creates an internal civil war (which existing customers get the new price? who gets to keep the old one?). They will move slowly. We have a 12–24 month protected wedge.

**Where we don't compete:** AxisCare's API breadth, mid-market 100–200 ADC sweet spot, partner-stitched integration ecosystem. We cede those for now and win the 5–80 ADC band first. Mid-market is a Y2–Y3 expansion play, not a Y1 fight.

**Operational read:** prioritize Alora displacement *before* AxisCare's offensive spend ramps. Every Alora customer we win in Q3'26 is one not available to AxisCare in Q2'27.

## Q5. Are we late on AI? What's our credibility play?

**Recommended position: We were late on AI as recently as the pre-Q1 2026 framing. As of v1 GA we are on time — but only if AI ships in v1, not "future phases." The credibility play is implementation discipline, not model marketing.**

The honest assessment from [01] §2.6: AI was a **parity (currently behind)** axis. The original v2 bundle put Claude API integration in "future phases." That framing is dead. Every Tier-1 incumbent has shipped a named AI product. Compassus deployed ambient AI to 8K+ clinicians. Customer expectation has shifted from "cool if you have it" to "why don't you have it."

**The shift:** AI is no longer a differentiator we can earn by shipping. It's a credibility-preserver we lose by *not* shipping. Treating it as a future-phase ships v1 with a known credibility gap on the single fastest-moving competitive axis in the category.

**The wedge — what no Tier-1 vendor has done:**

1. **AI included at every tier, including Starter $349.** AxisCare Intelligence, AlayaCare Layla, and Aaniie's Pro-tier AI are all add-ons or top-tier-only. The "AI included at every tier" position is *literally unoccupied in the lane* and is our cleanest single-feature pricing differentiator. (See [06] §2.3 pattern 3.)
2. **Implementation discipline, not model marketing.** Lead with PHI redaction middleware, audit logging of every model call (prompt + version + token spend), per-task model selection (Haiku for cheap structured work, Sonnet for documentation, Opus for escalations only), and reviewable/editable AI output. The marketing line is *"AI as DNA, not SKU,"* not *"powered by Claude."*
3. **Provider-neutral abstraction layer from day one.** Anthropic SDK in v1, but the abstraction lets us swap providers if pricing or availability shifts. Single-provider lock-in is a Y2 risk — modeled in [06] §10.4.

**Operational read:** AI documentation assist + AI scheduling assist + AI care-plan assist must ship in v1 GA, not v1.1. Per-customer infra cost model in [06] §5 already includes this; the cost is bounded (~$0.85/active client/mo blended). The engineering risk is real but manageable. **Slipping AI to v1.1 = shipping with a known competitive gap. Do not do this.**

## Q6. Why per-active-client pricing? What if customers hate it?

The single sharpest competitive weapon we have. Per-seat pricing on a 79%-turnover workforce is the structural failure mode incumbents won't fix because their LP economics depend on it. Every other unit was considered and rejected for explicit reasons (see [06] §6 — ten alternatives, ten rejections):

- **Per-caregiver-seat:** arms ShiftCare-pattern complaint corpus against us. Rejected.
- **Per-office-user:** caps revenue ceiling at ~$400–600/mo for a 200-ADC agency. Below ARPA floor. Rejected.
- **Revenue-share:** variable monthly cost is operationally hostile to thin-margin agencies; collection complexity. Rejected.
- **Per-visit:** bill volatility + perverse documentation incentive. Rejected.
- **Free-plus-paid:** adverse selection in a category where the unit of trial is the agency, not the user. Rejected.
- **Module unbundling (AlayaCare pattern):** the documented complaint corpus's #4 failure mode. Refused on principle.

**On customer hate:** the projected-bill view in the admin dashboard (updated daily, with overage notice the moment a tier threshold crosses) inverts the per-seat-vs-per-active-client predictability narrative. Active client counts change slowly. Caregiver headcount churns 79%/yr. Our bill is *more* predictable than per-seat. Defended in [06] §9.5.

## Q7. The trust pledges (never-raise + annual disclosure) — what are they really costing us?

**Pricing-power optionality on existing customers, traded for a defensible trust moat.**

- **Foregone revenue (modeled):** at 5% annual price escalation on existing customers (Toast/ServiceTitan default), foregone Y2–Y3 revenue is **$50K–$200K depending on scenario**. (See [06] §10.2.)
- **Asymmetric value gained:** lower CAC (the published pledge converts evaluators), lower churn (existing customers don't shop their renewal), and a brand position competitors *structurally cannot copy* without exposing PE-investor-sensitive data.
- **Annual disclosure cost:** ~1 PDF/year. Aggregated and ranged so we don't gift competitors per-customer cost data. Net operational cost: trivial.
- **Reassessment trigger:** Series B (Y3+). At that scale, list-price evolution on new customers (which the pledge allows) provides enough pricing power. Existing-customer pledges are still net positive on retention.

The trust architecture is also the **single most contrarian piece of the pricing page** and the highest-asymmetry brand commitment we make. It is non-negotiable.

## Q8. What's our most likely failure mode? What would make us kill this?

**Most likely failure modes, in descending probability:**

1. **Inbound funnel underperforms.** The whole pricing/CAC math depends on the published-pricing page acting as the funnel. If Year 1 CAC creeps past $5K on Starter/Growth, the LTV/CAC math breaks. **Mitigation:** SEO + paid content from day one; SDR coverage on Alora named-account list; minimum CAC payback target of 6 months on Starter, 12 months on Growth.
2. **AI ships late or unstable.** Already addressed in Q5. **Mitigation:** P1 engineering protection of the AI workstream; provider-neutral abstraction; v1 ships with documented degradation modes (no AI = manual templates still work).
3. **Caregiver mobile adoption underperforms.** Caregivers are the highest-volume / lowest-patience users. If WAU < 70% by month 3, the moat thesis fails. **Mitigation:** caregiver onboarding-flow design partner work pre-launch; activation-within-7-days as a P0 metric; quarterly app-store rating gate.
4. **AxisCare matches faster than expected.** Modeled in [06] §9.1. **Mitigation:** trust architecture (which they cannot match) + speed in the Alora window.

**Kill criteria (would prompt strategic reassessment):**

- < 10 paid logos by Month 6 post-GA (signals funnel failure)
- < 50% caregiver WAU by Month 3 (signals mobile-first thesis is false)
- Alora displacement window closes inside 4 months (LivTech communicates aggressively; signals our highest-leverage outbound motion is dead)
- AI documentation acceptance < 40% (signals AI-as-DNA narrative isn't landing — fix and re-baseline)

## Q9. What aren't we building, and why does that discipline matter?

The disqualified list (per [05] §8 and [01] §2.1):

- Medicare-certified skilled HH (OASIS-E2, PDGM, HHVBP) — HCHB/WellSky lane, multi-year compliance build
- Pure RCM / billing-as-a-service — entrenched scale players
- EVV aggregator / state Medicaid gateway — HHAeXchange moat
- Real-time caregiver GPS — product anti-pattern (caregiver respect is a brand attribute)
- Franchise corporate-office offering — WellSky 80% lock
- Sub-$100/mo ultra-small tier — unit economics don't support it
- Enterprise multi-state custom workflow engines — AlayaCare/AxisCare territory
- Pediatric / hospice / behavioral health workflows — adjacencies, not v1
- International — US-only in v1
- Hospital-at-home acute clinical — v2+ at earliest
- Human-in-the-loop / white-glove services revenue — product-led posture only

**Why the discipline matters:** every excluded category is one we will be tempted to chase when an enterprise-shaped lead comes in. The discipline is what protects the SMB wedge. **Default answer to "should we add X for this one big customer" is no.**

**Exit thesis** (per [04]):

**Strategic exit window:** **2026–2027 is the optimal window** before the 2025 sponsor vintage matures and competing processes crowd the market.
