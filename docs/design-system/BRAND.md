# COREcare Brand Guidelines

## Personality

COREcare is **warm, calming, and professional**. The platform serves people in stressful caregiving situations — both professional caregivers managing demanding shift schedules and family members anxious about their loved ones' care. Every design decision should reduce anxiety, build trust, and project competence.

### Brand Attributes

| Attribute | Expression |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Warm** | Soft, rounded UI elements. Friendly copy. Soothing color palette with organic greens. |
| **Calming** | Generous whitespace. Low-contrast backgrounds. Gentle animations. No aggressive reds for non-critical states. |
| **Professional** | Clean typography. Structured layouts. Consistent iconography. Medical-grade trustworthiness. |
| **Accessible** | High-contrast text. Large touch targets. Screen reader support. Inclusive language. |

## Color Rationale

### Primary: Soothing Greens
Green evokes health, growth, and calm. The primary palette uses muted, natural greens (not neon or saturated) to feel organic and trustworthy — like a well-kept garden, not a tech startup.

### Accents
- **Lavender** — Soft purple for secondary actions, selected states, and AI-powered features. Conveys intelligence and calm.
- **Coral** — Warm orange-red for alerts, urgency indicators, and calls to action. Used sparingly — only when something truly needs attention.
- **Sky** — Light blue for informational elements, links, and neutral highlights. Familiar and non-threatening.

### Neutrals
Warm grays (slightly blue-tinted) for text and backgrounds. Never pure black (#000) or pure white (#fff) — always slightly warm to maintain the organic feel.

## Voice & Tone

| Context | Voice |
|---------|-------|
| Success states | Warm, encouraging: "Visit documented successfully" |
| Error states | Calm, actionable: "We couldn't save your changes. Check your connection and try again." |
| Empty states | Helpful, forward-looking: "No shifts scheduled yet. Your manager will post shifts here." |
| Notifications | Brief, clear: "New shift available: Mon 8am-2pm" |
| Loading states | Reassuring: "Loading your schedule..." |

### HIPAA-Safe Copy

Never include PHI in:
- Push notification text
- Email subject lines
- Error messages
- Log output
- URL parameters

Use role-based references: "your client" not "John Smith", "upcoming visit" not "medication administration".

## Logo Usage

The COREcare wordmark uses the display font (serif) in primary-700 green. Minimum clear space is 1x the height of the "C". Minimum size is 24px height for digital use.
